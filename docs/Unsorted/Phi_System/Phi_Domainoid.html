section ‹Motivation \& Introduction›

text ‹The section covers two mechanisms about reasoning the separation disjunction between two given assertions,
i.e., (for the first mechanism) whether all of, and (for the second mechanism) whether exists some,
two objects ‹u ⊨ A› and ‹v ⊨ B› satisfying the given assertions ‹A› and ‹B›,
have defined separation operation between them, denoted by ‹u ## v› following the paper by Calcagno.
The motivation to infer such compatibility is based on two reasons.

▸ The first mechanism focuses on the following property,
  ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ ψ X Y› and ‹Separation_Disj⇩φ ψ D T U›
›

definition Sep_Disj :: ‹('a::sep_magma) BI ⇒ 'a BI ⇒ bool›
  where ‹Sep_Disj X Y ⟷ (∀u v. u ⊨ X ∧ v ⊨ Y ⟶ u ## v)›

definition Separation_Disj⇩ψ :: ‹('a::sep_magma ⇒ 'b::sep_magma) ⇒ 'a BI ⇒ 'a BI ⇒ bool›
  where ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ ψ X Y ⟷ (∀u v. u ⊨ X ∧ v ⊨ Y ∧ ψ u ## ψ v ⟶ u ## v)›

definition Separation_Disj⇩φ :: ‹('ca::sep_magma ⇒ 'cb::sep_magma) ⇒ ('ax × 'ay) set ⇒ ('ca, 'ax) φ ⇒ ('ca, 'ay) φ ⇒ bool›
  where ‹Separation_Disj⇩φ ψ D T U ⟷ (∀x y. (x,y) ∈ D ⟶ Separation_Disj⇩ψ ψ (x ⦂ T) (y ⦂ U))›


declare [[
φreason_default_pattern
      ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ ?ψ ?A ?B› ⇒ ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ ?ψ ?A ?B› (100)
  and ‹Separation_Disj⇩φ ?ψ _ ?T ?W› ⇒ ‹Separation_Disj⇩φ ?ψ _ ?T _› (130)
]]

text ‹
  The standard homomorphism from a partial algebra ‹𝒜› to another ‹ℬ› only assumes the group operation
  defined (between two certain elements) in ‹𝒜›, is also defined in ‹ℬ›, but not reversely, i.e.,
  ‹u ## v ⟶ ψ(u) ## ψ(v)   but not generally   ψ(u) ## ψ(v) ⟶ u ## v ›
  It blocks the ‹x ⦂ (ψ ⨾ T) ∗ (ψ ⨾ U) 𝗍𝗋𝖺𝗇𝗌𝖿𝗈𝗋𝗆𝗌 zip(x) ⦂ (ψ ⨾ T ∗ U)›, one side of the φ-type separation homomorphism.

  Certainly, to circumvent it, we can ask a stronger assumption i.e. closed homomorphism,
  but not all homomorphisms are closed. An example is super permission...

  As a remedy, ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ A B› allows the φ-type transformation for non-closed separation homomorphism.

  To enable ‹x ⦂ (ψ ⨾ T) ∗ (ψ ⨾ U) 𝗍𝗋𝖺𝗇𝗌𝖿𝗈𝗋𝗆𝗌 zip(x) ⦂ ψ ⨾ (T ∗ U)›, the weakest condition is
  ‹SD⇩ψ'(A,B) ⟷ (∀u v. u ⊨ A ∧ v ⊨ B ∧ ψ u ## ψ v ⟶ ∃u' v'. u' ⊨ A ∧ v' ⊨ B ∧ ψ(u) * ψ(v) = ψ(u') * ψ(v') ∧ u' ## v')›
  However, ‹SD⇩ψ'(A,B)› is difficult to automate and ‹ψ(u) * ψ(v) = ψ(u') * ψ(v')› is hard to deal.
  We fail to find a reasoning rule splitting ‹SD⇩ψ'(A, B⇩1 ∗ B⇩2)› to the respective cases for ‹B⇩1› and ‹B⇩2›.
  Due to this, we apply an approximation assuming the ‹u',v'› are equal to the ‹u,v›, and then
  we get the form of ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ› and it has simpler reasoning rules such as
  ‹ Separation_Disj⇩ψ ψ A B⇩1
⟹ Separation_Disj⇩ψ ψ A B⇩2
⟹ Separation_Disj⇩ψ ψ A (B⇩1 * B⇩2)› for separation homomorphism ‹ψ›.

  See §‹Domainoid gives Separation_Disj⇩ψ›

▸ The second mechanism focuses on satisfaction of multiplicative conjunction, of the following form,
  ‹Satisfiable A ⟹ Satisfiable B ⟹ with_what_condition ⟶ Satisfiable (A * B)›

  The implication reasoning ‹Satisfiable A ⟶ P› infers a weaker but simpler approximation
  of the pure fact implied inside ‹A›.
  However, only the weakening part is not enough for φ-types of mappings such as
  ‹f ⦂ T ⇛ U := {g. (∀u x. u ⊨ x ⦂ T ⟶ g(y) ⊨ f(x) ⦂ U)}› (forward simulation)
  whose domain φ-type ‹T› is contravariant.
  To extract its implication, the dual of the implication reasoning is required, namely satisfaction reasoning.
  It infers a stronger approximation ‹Q› such that ‹Q ⟶ Satisfiable A› for a given assertion ‹A›.
  By it, we can complete the implication rule of ‹f ⦂ T ⇛ U›,
  ‹ (⋀x. Q x ⟶ Satisfiable (x ⦂ T))
⟹ (⋀x. Satisfiable (x ⦂ T) → P x)
⟹ (⋀y. Satisfiable (y ⦂ U) → P' y)
⟹ Satisfiable (f ⦂ T ⇛ U) ⟶ (∀x. Q x ⟶ P x ∧ P' (f x))›.
  
  The rules of satisfaction reasoning for logical connectives are given where???, except those
  for conjunctive operators (‹∧› and ‹*›).
  For ‹∧›, it is due to that the inhabitance of each side of the conjunction
  does not imply the inhabitance of the both sides, because the residents may not equal.
  For ‹*›, it is due to, though we have two residents ‹u ⊨ A› and ‹v ⊨ B› for each assertions,
  we do not know if ‹u ## v›, so we cannot deduce ‹u * v ⊨ A * B›.

  Even though ‹∧› is less-intuitive and rarely used under our interpretation of data refinement,
  multiplicative conjunction is essential,
  especially for example, when the key of the map is a tuple and the tuple fields are connected by ‹*›.

  See §‹Domainoid gives Satisfaction of Separation Conjunction›
›

text ‹
The satisfaction reasoning of multiplicative conjunctions, together with the above ‹Separation_Disj⇩ψ›,
relies on the notion of ∗‹domainoid›, which is invented in order to reason the separation disjunction
from the abstract side of φ-types, particularly by means of ‹Domainoid_Homo› presented later.
›


section ‹The Algebra of Domainoid›

subsection ‹Domainoid›

type_synonym ('a,'d) domainoid = ‹ 'a ⇒ 'd ›
  ― ‹A forgetful functor that extracts out domains and trims off data›

definition domainoid :: ‹'a itself ⇒ ('a::sep_magma,'d::sep_magma) domainoid ⇒ bool›
  where ‹domainoid _ δ ⟷ closed_homo_sep δ›

text ‹A domainoid extraction ‹δ› is a closed homomorphism and also a forgetful functor that extracts the domain
  parts and forgets the data parts of a SA, such that ‹δ(x) ## δ(y) ⟷ x ## y› is sufficient to
  determine the separation disjunction ‹x ## y›, but of a simpler expression.
  Usually, ‹δ(x)› is the domain of the resource ‹x›, such as the address of a memory object,
  but it can be others like ‹address → permission› the permission map of a sharing memory
  ‹address → permission × value›. Considering this, we call it domain-oid extraction or simply domainoid.

  The domainoid of an algebra ‹A› can be defined as the terminal object of the coslice
  ‹CH_Group/A› of the category ‹CH_Group› of closed homomorphisms, i.e., the homomorphism to
  the minimal algebra that just preserves the domain of the group operation of ‹A›.
  However, as the domainoid only means to be an intermediate representation helping the reasoning, we
  relax the definition to be any closed homomorphism that helps evaluating the separation disjunction.

  By extracting the domainoids of two φ-type assertions or other assertions, we can determine the
  separation disjunction between the two assertions without involving and reducing to
  concrete representations of the resources, as usually we only abstract the data and leave the
  resource identifiers untouched.

  With abbreviation ‹domainoid d ≜ closed_homo_sep δ› we emphasize ‹δ› is a domainoid.

  Modality ‹𝔇[d] S ≜ (δ u ⦂ Itself 𝗌𝗎𝖻𝗃 u. u ⊨ S)› for domaionoid extraction ‹δ› maps
  an assertion ‹S› to the domainoids of its resources, specified still by a BI assertion.
  The modality is homomorphic over all logical connectives except additive conjunctions (including universal quantification).
  Though domainoid is designed to solve satisfaction of multiplicative conjunction, it still can do nothing
  to additive conjunctions.

  For mapping the domainoid functor onto the abstract domain of a φ-type ‹x ⦂ T›,
  there are lower and upper homomorphisms giving stronger and lower approximations respectively
  for domainoid ‹d›,
  ‹Domainoid_Homo⇩L d T d' ⟷ (∀x. d' x ⟶ Ψ[d] (x ⦂ T) )›
  ‹Domainoid_Homo⇩U d T d' ⟷ (∀x. Ψ[d] (x ⦂ T) ⟶ d' x )›
  We use approximations in case of the precise expression is too complicated.

  The lower homomorphism is used for deducing the satisfaction of multiplicative conjunction.
  The upper homomorphism is for enabling transformation of non-closed separation homomorphism.
›

lemma [φreason %extract_pure]:
  ‹ 𝗋EIF (closed_homo_sep δ) P
⟹ 𝗋EIF (domainoid TY δ) P ›
  unfolding domainoid_def
  by blast

lemma [φreason %extract_pure]:
  ‹ 𝗋ESC P (closed_homo_sep δ)
⟹ 𝗋ESC P (domainoid TY δ) ›
  unfolding Action_Tag_def domainoid_def
  by blast

subsubsection ‹Domainoid gives Satisfaction of Separation Conjunction›

lemma [φreason 1000]:
  ‹ Pa 𝗌𝗎𝖿𝖿𝗂𝖼𝖾𝗌 A
⟹ Pb 𝗌𝗎𝖿𝖿𝗂𝖼𝖾𝗌 B
⟹ domainoid TYPE('c::sep_magma) δ
⟹ (𝖼𝗈𝗇𝖽𝗂𝗍𝗂𝗈𝗇 (closed_homo_sep δ ∧ Satisfiable A) ⟹ A' ≤ Ψ[domainoid_tag δ] A) ―‹expand ‹Ψ[d] A, Ψ[d] B› to a simpler (but should still strong) upper approximation›
⟹ (𝖼𝗈𝗇𝖽𝗂𝗍𝗂𝗈𝗇 (closed_homo_sep δ ∧ Satisfiable B) ⟹ B' ≤ Ψ[domainoid_tag δ] B)
⟹ 𝗀𝗎𝖺𝗋𝖽 𝖼𝗈𝗇𝖽𝗂𝗍𝗂𝗈𝗇 (Pa ∧ Pb ⟶ (∃a b. a ⊨ A' ∧ b ⊨ B' ∧ a ## b))
⟹ Pa ∧ Pb 𝗌𝗎𝖿𝖿𝗂𝖼𝖾𝗌 A * B›
  unfolding Satisfiable_def BI_sub_iff Premise_def 𝗋EIF_def 𝗋ESC_def
            domainoid_def domainoid_tag_def 𝗋Guard_def
  by (clarsimp simp add: closed_homo_sep_def closed_homo_sep_disj_def; blast)


lemma ― ‹The above rule is reversible for any domainoid extraction ‹d››
  ‹ domainoid TYPE('c::sep_magma) d
⟹ Satisfiable (A * B) ⟷ (∃a b. a ⊨ Ψ[d] A ∧ b ⊨ Ψ[d] B ∧ a ## b)›
  unfolding Satisfiable_def
  by (clarsimp simp add: domainoid_def closed_homo_sep_def closed_homo_sep_disj_def; blast)


subsubsection ‹Domainoid gives Separation_Disj›


lemma [φreason default 10]:
  ‹ domainoid TYPE('c::sep_magma) δ
⟹ domainoid TYPE('c⇩ψ::sep_magma) δ⇩ψ
⟹ (⋀x. 𝖼𝗈𝗇𝖽𝗂𝗍𝗂𝗈𝗇 closed_homo_sep δ ⟹ Ψ[domainoid_tag δ] (x ⦂ T) ≤ 𝔇⇩T x)
⟹ (⋀y. 𝖼𝗈𝗇𝖽𝗂𝗍𝗂𝗈𝗇 closed_homo_sep δ ⟹ Ψ[domainoid_tag δ] (y ⦂ U) ≤ 𝔇⇩U y)
⟹ fun_commute δ⇩ψ ψ δ ψ⇩D ∧⇩𝗋 has_ψ⇩D = True ∨⇩c⇩u⇩t has_ψ⇩D = False

definition domainoid_mapper :: ‹'c⇩1 itself ⇒ 'c⇩2 itself
                            ⇒ ('c⇩1::sep_magma, 'd⇩1::sep_magma) domainoid
                            ⇒ ('c⇩2::sep_magma, 'd⇩2::sep_magma) domainoid
                            ⇒ bool ›
  where ‹domainoid_mapper T⇩1 T⇩2 δ⇩1 δ⇩2 ⟷ (domainoid T⇩1 δ⇩1 ⟶ domainoid T⇩2 δ⇩2)›

definition comm_domainoid_mapper :: ‹ 'c⇩1 itself ⇒ 'c⇩2 itself
                                  ⇒ ('c⇩1::sep_magma, 'd⇩1::sep_magma) domainoid
                                  ⇒ ('c⇩2::sep_magma, 'd⇩2::sep_magma) domainoid
                                  ⇒ ('d⇩1 ⇒ 'd⇩2) ⇒ ('c⇩1 ⇒ 'c⇩2)
                                  ⇒ bool›
  where ‹comm_domainoid_mapper T⇩1 T⇩2 δ⇩1 δ⇩2 f⇩1 f⇩2 ⟷ domainoid_mapper T⇩1 T⇩2 δ⇩1 δ⇩2 ∧
                                                   (closed_homo_sep δ⇩1 ⟶ fun_commute f⇩1 δ⇩1 f⇩2 δ⇩2) ›

definition comm_domainoid_mapper_rev :: ‹ 'c⇩1 itself ⇒ 'c⇩2 itself
                                  ⇒ ('c⇩1::sep_magma, 'd⇩1::sep_magma) domainoid
                                  ⇒ ('c⇩2::sep_magma, 'd⇩2::sep_magma) domainoid
                                  ⇒ ('c⇩2 ⇒ 'c⇩1) ⇒ ('d⇩2 ⇒ 'd⇩1)
                                  ⇒ bool›
  where ‹comm_domainoid_mapper_rev T⇩1 T⇩2 δ⇩1 δ⇩2 f⇩1 f⇩2 ⟷ domainoid_mapper T⇩1 T⇩2 δ⇩1 δ⇩2 ∧
                                                       (closed_homo_sep δ⇩1 ⟶ fun_commute δ⇩1 f⇩1 δ⇩2 f⇩2) ›

declare [[
  φreason_default_pattern_ML ‹domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 ?T⇩2 ?δ⇩1 ?δ⇩2› ⇒
    ‹fn ctxt => fn prop as Trueprop $ (dom_mapper $ T1 $ T2 $ d1 $ d2) =>
     let fun chk (\<^Const>‹Pure.type ‹TVar _››, d) = is_Var d
           | chk (T,d) = is_Var T andalso is_Var d
         val idx = Term.maxidx_of_term prop + 1
         val rewr = the_list oo Pattern_Translation.rewrite (Context.theory_of ctxt) (K false) [] o Logic.dest_conjunction
         val ret = (if chk (T1,d1) then [] else rewr \<^pattern>‹domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 ?T⇩2 ?δ⇩1 ?δ⇩2 &&& domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 _ ?δ⇩1 _› prop)
                 @ (if chk (T2,d2) then [] else rewr \<^pattern>‹domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 ?T⇩2 ?δ⇩1 ?δ⇩2 &&& domainoid_mapper _ ?T⇩2 _ ?δ⇩2› prop)
                 @ rewr \<^pattern>‹domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 ?T⇩2 ?δ⇩1 ?δ⇩2 &&& domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 ?T⇩2 _ _› prop
      in SOME ret
     end› (100),

  φreason_default_pattern ‹comm_domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 _ ?δ⇩1 _ ?f⇩1 _ › ⇒
                          ‹comm_domainoid_mapper ?T⇩1 _ ?δ⇩1 _ ?f⇩1 _ ›    (100)
                      and ‹comm_domainoid_mapper_rev _ ?T⇩2 _ ?δ⇩2 _ ?f⇩2 › ⇒
                          ‹comm_domainoid_mapper_rev _ ?T⇩2 _ ?δ⇩2 _ ?f⇩2 ›    (100)
]]
